Biryani Master Class

A step-by-step interactive guide to making perfect biryani

Rice

2 cups basmati rice
Water for soaking
1 tbsp salt (for boiling)
2 bay leaves
4 green cardamom
4 cloves
1 inch cinnamon stick

Meat / Protein

500g chicken or goat
1 cup yogurt
2 onions, sliced thin
2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
2 green chilies
1 tsp red chili powder
1 tsp turmeric
1 tsp garam masala
Salt to taste

Layering & Finishing

Pinch of saffron in warm milk
2 tbsp ghee
Fresh mint leaves
Fresh cilantro
Fried onions (birista)
1 tsp kewra water (optional)

Preparation

1
Wash & soak rice in cold water for 30 minutes. This helps grains cook long and separate.
2
Marinate the meat with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, salt, half the mint and cilantro. Rest at least 1 hour (overnight is best).
3
Fry onions in oil until deep golden-brown and crispy. Set aside — these are your birista.

Cooking

4
Par-boil the rice with whole spices (bay leaves, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon) in salted water until 70% cooked. Drain immediately.
5
Cook the marinated meat on medium heat until it's 80% done and the masala has reduced. About 15–20 minutes.
6
Layer it up: Spread meat on the bottom. Add a layer of par-boiled rice. Sprinkle fried onions, mint, cilantro, saffron milk, ghee. Repeat if enough rice.
7
Dum (steam cook): Seal the pot with foil or dough, cover with a tight lid. Cook on high heat for 3 minutes, then very low heat for 25 minutes. Do NOT open the lid.
8
Rest & serve: Turn off heat, let it rest 5 minutes. Gently mix layers bottom-to-top. Garnish with remaining fried onions and serve with raita.

Secrets to Perfect Biryani

Always use aged basmati. Aged rice absorbs less water and stays longer — look for "extra long grain" aged 1–2 years.
Don't skip the soak. 30 minutes minimum. This pre-hydrates the grain so it cooks evenly and won't break.
70% rule for rice. Par-boil until each grain is soft outside but has a tiny chalky core when you bite it. It finishes on dum.
Low and slow dum. After the initial 3 min on high, use the lowest flame possible. Place a tawa (flat pan) under the pot to diffuse heat.
Seal matters. Use aluminum foil under the lid, or a flour-water dough seal. Steam must stay trapped inside.
Onions = flavor backbone. Fry them low and slow until mahogany brown. Burnt onions will make the biryani bitter.
Saffron hack: Soak a few strands in 2 tbsp warm milk for 15 min. Drizzle only on the top layer for color contrast.
Rest before serving. 5 minutes off heat lets the steam redistribute. The bottom layer finishes cooking in residual heat.
Gentle mixing. Use a flat spatula and fold from the edges inward, bottom to top. Never stir — you'll mush the rice.
